The Journey

I discovered hip-hop at the age of 12 and started writing my own lyrics. Three years later, I was performing at Antwerp's legendary club "Funk You" (1981-1995), where I learned one of the most valuable lessons of my career: music isn't just something you hear...it's something you feel.

Those nights taught me about energy, connection, and the relationship between an artist and an audience. Looking back, they were my first lessons in music production, even before I ever stepped into a recording studio.

After serving five years in the Belgian military in Lüdenscheid, Germany, I made the decision to dedicate my life to music. Returning to Belgium, I was given the opportunity to produce a track for the late Tony McKenzie (R.I.P), the owner of Funk You. That recording eventually reached the producer of Technotronic, leading to my first professional release as BC & The Basic Boom in 1990.

That moment marked the beginning of a journey that has now lasted more than 35 years.

As the years went by, my role evolved naturally. I started as an artist, became a songwriter, moved into production, and eventually discovered a passion for mixing and mastering. I began mixing my own records because I couldn't find anyone who captured the sound I heard in my head.

One of the defining chapters of my career began with "Tweak", my collaboration with Felix da Housecat featuring Leki. That project led me to Atlanta, Georgia, where I lived and worked with Felix for approximately five years. During that time, I learned that great dance music isn't created by software alone. It comes from understanding people, DJs, clubs, and the energy of a dance floor.

Alongside my work as Tha Lunaman, I continued producing and developing artists, including Leki, always believing that a producer's role is to help an artist discover the strongest version of their own identity. More recently, I've had the privilege of working with Tourist LeMC, collaborating as Co-Producer, Mixing Engineer, and Mastering Engineer.

"I have never made music to become famous."

I have never believed that artists are more important than anyone else. We're all people with different talents and passions. What has always driven me is the love of creating music and the satisfaction of helping artists bring their ideas to life.

Because I started as an artist myself, I understand the trust it takes to place your music in someone else's hands. I never take that trust for granted. After more than 35 years, I still approach every project with the same curiosity I had when I first walked into Funk You as a teenager.
